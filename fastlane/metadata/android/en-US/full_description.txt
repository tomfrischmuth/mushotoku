Mushotoku (無所得) is a Zen term for acting without grasping for a result. It's a deliberately odd name for a productivity app — and that's the point.

Most productivity apps want you to optimize every minute and defend every streak. Mushotoku goes the other way: work out the few things that actually matter today, do them, and let the rest go. It's a tool for focusing, not for filling.

Everything you write, track, and plan stays on your device. Mushotoku ships with no INTERNET permission at all, so it physically cannot send your data anywhere — the option was never built. Your data is stored encrypted on the device.

Features:

Plan & organize
* Calendar with appointments and an offline holiday layer
* Tasks to manage your to-dos
* Habits you can build and track

Capture & reflect
* Notes with markdown editor, a clean read view and a trash with restore
* Gratitude journal with a searchable archive
* Mood tracking over time

Wellbeing
* Meditation timer with three bells and Buddhist quotes
* Track caffeine intake and see how much caffeine is still active at bedtime

Money
* Finance tracker with custom categories
* Detailed reports and savings potential
